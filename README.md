# Introduction
If you’re reading this, you’ve been invited to—or are curious about—this mod pack. It fuses game mechanics from a wide range of titles, all driven by two common goals:
1) building factories, automating processes, and mastering logistics;
2) player-driven military, economic, and diplomatic interaction, including armed conflict.
While adapting these mechanics, our vision was to find a balance and a meaningful connection between those two overarching ideas.

# Inspiration
The core of this pack was born from countless hours in games like Factorio, Sid Meier’s Civilization VI, Satisfactory, Eco, Endless Space, Trailmakers, and War Thunder. These games offer brilliant mechanics: effort-based science, player and faction specialisations, and deeply interwoven player interactions. Yet they all lack the freedom and boundless creativity that Minecraft provides—a gap this mod pack aims to fill.

# Game Mechanics Implementation
The very heart of this pack comes from Factorio: its effort-driven science and tech tree. By implementing this core mechanic, we want to motivate players to invest in automation, which in turn opens up more room for creativity and interaction. This is realised through the Create mod’s factory automation, paired with an extensive FTB Quests technology and quest tree. The quest tree covers the majority of the mods’ content, giving experienced players enough time to set up advanced facilities while offering newcomers plenty of space to explore and learn.

Once automation meets most basic needs, players are free to pursue self-expression. Cultural activities—heavily inspired by Sid Meier’s Civilization VI and Eco—let achievements in architecture, music, visual arts, and trade contribute to your ultimate goal of becoming the most technologically advanced faction. The tools for these cultural feats come in the form of decorative blocks, musical instruments, paintings, photography, and player-organised events.

But all of that holds only until peace is no longer an option. Alongside cultural enrichment and factory building, our mod pack includes many mechanics and mods for armed conflict, whether to settle disputes or to pursue total domination. In the end, the winner is the last one standing—from a civilisation’s point of view. Even so, you are always welcome to form alliances, trade unions, and wield diplomacy as your finest weapon.

# Core Mods and In-Depth Mechanics
## Create and Create: The Factory Must Grow
These are the foundational mods that shape the entire pack and deliver its critical gameplay. At their core, they automate the boring stuff and generate passive resource income. As the creators of this pack, however, we want to push these mechanics even further and nudge players toward automating *everything* they can.

## Create: Aeronautics
This recent Create add-on practically stands as a mod of its own, bringing realistic physics to Minecraft and expanding Create’s mechanical possibilities. Players can construct physically accurate portable machinery, vehicles, and more—limited only by their imagination. In this mod pack, Aeronautics isn’t just about better travel and mobile machines (like tunnel borers); it also opens up new dimensions for non-peaceful negotiations, both in the air and at sea.

## Create: Big Cannons and Timeless and Classics: Zero
These mods add a staggering array of tools in various calibres, perfect for making your territorial claims very clear to your neighbours. Because your neighbours will have access to the same tools, you’ll need to think about personal safety *and* the safety of your entire estate. It’s the classic battle of sword and shield, dressed in a more modern style.

# FYI
We understand that our vision leaves less room for the classic Minecraft experience. But that’s exactly what we value in this mod pack: the chance to combine Minecraft’s flexibility and creativity with the other games we love. In essence, this creates a new game—one that truly has no equal.
